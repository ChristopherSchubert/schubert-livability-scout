Slovenia, planned

a step-by-step walkthrough · for Janice's review · ← → to move 1 /

She does:

arrow keys work

It starts on the Trips list with two facts in their heads — Slovenia, mid-May-ish — and one button.

The composer: where, a provisional date range (a calendar can't lay out “mid-May” — concrete dates, explicitly rough), and travelers as rows. Janice's row needs its chip — she taps ＋ chip.

The picker opens — the same marker taxonomy used everywhere in the app, plus custom. She picks 🥦 for Janice, then for Chris. A chip added here follows its traveler onto every screen of the trip.

The composer is complete — where, provisional dates, two chipped travelers. Create trip is all that's left, and the next screen is the trip itself.

The trip opens on its Plan tab with the provisional window already drawn — May 15–25 came in from the composer. Both edges slide freely (nothing is booked); ✎ adjust dates is the value-editor for the same thing. Cities wait below.

✎ adjust dates opens the value editor for the same thing the edges drag — fields and handles always agree. Until something books, both stay free.

Three cities dropped in rough order — the split is a default, 2 · 4 · 4 nights. Kranj stays on the tray. Every bar has its grip; every boundary its handle.

＋ other city opens a place search — any city resolves, not just the scout's list. (Trieste enters the story here; it returns in the variations finale.) An added city lands on the tray as a chip, ready to drag in.

Zhuzhing: grab the boundary handle and slide — Bled takes a night from Piran. Zero-sum between neighbors; the trip total doesn't move.

＋ add transport opens the transport editor: route, each end's local clock, ticket fields that wait honestly empty until ticketed — and the consequence stated before it happens: booking this locks the trip's end date.

The flights get booked and the window's outer edges lock. The trip can't stretch anymore without real money moving. Inside, still clay.

Locked is not frozen: the lock holds only the outer edges. Inside, she drops Kranj in and trades nights freely. The lock's tooltip names its cause and its exit — change the return flight and the edge softens. Hardening is always reversible at the price of the thing that caused it.

Transport done, she works down the same Plan page to Stays — empty so far. 🔍 search hotels opens a place search scoped to the leg; ＋ shortlist collects candidates, with add-your-own for places no cache knows.

The shortlist from the search now sits under Ljubljana as the candidate strip (each card: ✎ · ✕). She drags Union onto the city — it becomes a bar spanning the leg's nights.

The same search-and-drop, twice more: Toplice for Bled, Hotel Piran for Piran. Three cities, three bars — every night housed, nothing split yet.

✂ split a stay asks one question — after which night? — and the split leaves an honest open slot (“night of May 20 — drop a stay”) rather than silently stretching a neighbor. Hiša Franko lands in that slot next.

Stays aren't 1:1 with cities: Hiša Franko — added by hand, the splurge — takes the open night. The same split-and-drop later hands the last night to Brdo, near the airport.

The stay bar's click opens the stay editor — this is where booking actually happens: status to booked, confirmation pasted, the free-cancellation date captured once and reused everywhere (the bar's resistance, the Book page's countdown, the ⏰ badge).

All five booked the same way. Booked bars wear a 🔒 and resist: grabbing Union's handle surfaces its terms — free cancellation ends May 12.

How you reach a city's bucket: the window is the navigation — click the Bled segment and the page focuses Bled's section: its stays, its days, and an empty bucket stating its three ways in (browse suggestions · add your own · booked-and-dated places itself).

How things get in: she browses the Bled suggestions (from Google Places near the stay) and presses + Save — eight land in the bucket, each carrying ✎ edit · ✕ remove. ~16 h gathered for ~3 open days. The thing no cache knows comes next.

＋ add your own opens the item editor for things no cache knows. The balloon gets its real fields — duration, vendor, confirmation, date+time, meeting point, wear — and the editor states the consequence: has a date → will be placed on Tue 19.

The ✎ on any card opens the same editor (duration and hours live here; cited markers are read-only). ✕ removes — with an Undo toast, never silently. She undoes it; the toboggan survives.

Janice already knows another answer: lunch at Rustika on balloon day. She drags it from the bucket straight onto Tue — manual placement works at any stage, and it lands pinned.

Some days are for nothing: May 22 gets the 🪶 keep-it-light toggle. The layout engine must leave it nearly empty. Recovery is a feature.

“Lay out Bled.” The machine deals all seven undated items around the two pins — closed-Mondays respected (Public & Vegan lands Sunday), the drive day kept light. The zipline doesn't fit and becomes an alternate, not a casualty.

She drags the toboggan from Monday onto Wednesday — pair it with the drive day. The instant a human edits, auto-layout pauses — and waits for the explicit “re-lay out around my pins.”

Tuesday looks right — Solve. The Days view: the machine sequenced by geography, inserted the drives, dropped lunch in its window, surfaced the free gap, assigned the clock.

This is the sheet the ✎ opened: one editor, every door — same fields here as on the Shelf. She types 12:30, and the moment a solved time changes, Hold at this time checks itself. Done returns to the day, now marked edited.

Back on the day: lunch pinned at 12:30, the day marked “edited since solve,” one button to re-solve around her pins. (Drag-to-retime belongs to the grid view — list rows don't pretend to support it.)

She tries the zipline on Tuesday anyway. The machine reflows — and refuses to lie: it names what can't fit and offers the next day instead of silently cramming.

The Book page kept itself: to-books with lead times, cancel-bys counting down, cash = on-site ∧ cash-only = €927, passes remembered. Nothing hand-kept.

“Mark booked…” opens the booking sheet: confirmation, date, money. The consequence chain is stated and automatic — dated → placed on Sun 24, booked → off the Needs-action list, on-site cost → into the cash tally.

The payoff: the grid prints itself. Every block computed from the plan, logistics one click deep, one leg per page on paper. The artifact Janice used to type — now output.

Day-of, the phone lands on today: next-up leads with Directions and Call, the confirmation code one thumb away, finished things faded.

＋ new variation opens the fork composer: name it, start blank or copy the current plan — and the decide-by rule is stated up front, derived from whatever cancellation deadlines end up inside.

The real story: they cancelled Piran betting on Trieste — and regretted it. Variations keep both futures alive on the same 3 days, each holding refundable bookings, with a decide-by inherited from the cancellation deadlines.
