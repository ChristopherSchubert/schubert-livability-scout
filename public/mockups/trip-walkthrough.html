    It starts on the Trips list with two facts in their heads — Slovenia, mid-May-ish — and one button.

    The composer: where, a provisional date range (a calendar can't lay out “mid-May” — concrete dates, explicitly rough), and travelers as rows. Janice's row needs its chip — you tap ＋ chip.

    The picker opens — the same marker taxonomy used everywhere in the app, plus custom. You pick 🥦 for Janice, then for Chris. A chip added here follows its traveler onto every screen of the trip.

    The composer is complete — where, provisional dates, two chipped travelers. Create trip is all that's left, and the next screen is the trip itself.

    The trip opens on its Plan tab with the provisional window already drawn — May 15–25 came in from the composer. Both edges slide freely (nothing is booked); ✎ adjust dates is the value-editor for the same thing. Cities wait below.

    The window’s dates edit two ways that always agree: drag the edges, or press ✎ adjust dates for the same values as fields.

    The From and To fields hold exactly what the edges drag — change either, they stay in sync. Until something books, both stay free; Done puts the editor away.

    Three cities dropped in rough order — the split is a default, 2 · 4 · 4 nights. Kranj stays on the tray. Every bar has its grip; every boundary its handle — and each city lands with its empty stay slot below (🔍 search lives there).

    ＋ other city opens a place search — any city resolves, not just the scout's list.

    Type, and Trieste resolves (it returns in the variations finale). Add it — the city lands on the tray as a chip, ready to drag in.

    Zhuzhing: grab the boundary handle and slide — Bled takes a night from Piran. Zero-sum between neighbors; the trip total doesn't move.

    The Transport section sits empty under the window. Press ＋ add transport to open the flight editor.

    ＋ add transport opens the transport editor: route, each end's local clock, ticket fields that wait honestly empty until ticketed — and the consequence stated before it happens: booking this locks the trip's end date.

    The flights get booked and the window's outer edges lock. The trip can't stretch anymore without real money moving. Inside, still clay.

    Locked is not frozen: the lock holds only the outer edges. Inside, you drop Kranj in and trade nights freely. The lock's tooltip names its cause and its exit — change the return flight and the edge softens. Hardening is always reversible at the price of the thing that caused it.

    Each empty stay slot offers 🔍 Search hotels (or a drop). Press Ljubljana's — it searches that one leg.

    Transport done, you fold it closed (▸) — and with the dates settled, the big calendar compacts to a strip (✎ adjust dates still opens it). Every empty stay slot has carried its 🔍 search since the cities landed — pressing Ljubljana's runs a place search scoped to that leg, and you shortlist all five results: the candidate strip on the next page is exactly these five. Add-your-own covers places no cache knows.

    The shortlist from the search now sits under Ljubljana as the candidate strip (each card: ✎ · ✕). You drag Union onto the city — it becomes a bar spanning the leg's nights.

    The same search-and-drop, twice more (a drop retires its candidate strip): Toplice for Bled, Hotel Piran for Piran. Three cities, three bars — every night housed, nothing split yet.

    Stays aren't 1:1 with cities — a single leg can hold two hotels. ✂ split a stay cuts one in two; press it on Toplice.

    ✂ split a stay asks one question — after which night? — and the split leaves an honest open slot (“night of May 20 — drop a stay”) rather than silently stretching a neighbor. Hiša Franko lands in that slot next.

    Stays aren't 1:1 with cities: Hiša Franko — added by hand, the splurge — takes the open night. The same split-and-drop later hands the last night to Brdo, near the airport.

    With every night housed, click a stay bar to book it — start with Union (Ljubljana). Its booking detail opens on the right.

    The stay bar's click opens the stay editor — this is where booking actually happens: status to booked, confirmation pasted, the free-cancellation date captured once and reused everywhere (the bar's resistance, the Book page's countdown, the ⏰ badge).

    All five booked the same way. Booked bars wear a 🔒 and resist: grabbing Union's handle surfaces its terms — free cancellation ends May 12.

    How you reach a city's bucket: the window is the navigation — click the Bled segment and the page focuses: Transport and Stays fold to bars, and Bled's section opens — its stays, its days, and an empty bucket stating its three ways in (browse suggestions · add your own · booked-and-dated places itself).

    How things get in: Browse suggestions → opens the tray right under the bucket (from Google Places near the stay); + Save moves a card up into it — eight saved, each carrying ✎ edit · ✕ remove. ~16 h gathered for ~3 open days. The thing no cache knows comes next.

    ＋ add your own opens the item editor for things no cache knows. The balloon gets its real fields — duration, vendor, confirmation, date+time, meeting point, wear — and the editor states the consequence: has a date → will be placed on Tue 19.

    The ✎ on any card opens the same editor (duration and hours live here; cited markers are read-only). ✕ removes — with an Undo toast, never silently. You undo it; the toboggan survives.

    You already know another answer: lunch at Rustika on balloon day. You drag it from the bucket straight onto Tue — manual placement works at any stage, and it lands pinned.

    Back on Bled, the bucket holds seven undated items plus the two pins — enough to lay out. Press Lay out Bled →.

    “Lay out Bled.” The machine deals all seven undated items around the two pins — closed-Mondays respected (Public & Vegan lands Sunday), the drive day kept light. The zipline doesn't fit and becomes an alternate, not a casualty.

    You drag the toboggan from Monday onto Wednesday — pair it with the drive day. The instant a human edits, auto-layout pauses — and waits for the explicit “re-lay out around my pins.”

    Tuesday looks right — Solve. The Days view: the machine sequenced by geography, inserted the drives, dropped lunch in its window, surfaced the free gap, assigned the clock.

    This is the sheet the ✎ opened: one editor, every door — same fields here as on the Shelf. You type 12:30, and the moment a solved time changes, Hold at this time checks itself. Done returns to the day, now marked edited.

    Back on the day: lunch pinned at 12:30, the day marked “edited since solve,” one button to re-solve around your pins. (Drag-to-retime belongs to the grid view — list rows don't pretend to support it.)

    You try the zipline on Tuesday anyway. The machine reflows — and refuses to lie: it names what can't fit and offers the next day instead of silently cramming.

    Bled is planned to the clock. Piran you keep lighter — click its segment, toggle 🪶 keep-it-light on May 22 so the engine leaves it nearly empty, and add the one errand by hand: decide the Postojna caves. Recovery is a feature.

    The Book page kept itself: to-books with lead times, cancel-bys counting down, cash = on-site ∧ cash-only = €927, passes remembered. Nothing hand-kept.

    “Mark booked…” opens the booking sheet: confirmation, date, money. The consequence chain is stated and automatic — dated → placed on Sun 24, booked → off the Needs-action list, on-site cost → into the cash tally.

    The payoff: the grid prints itself, one leg per page. Every block placed, every drive auto-timed and inserted (→ 4 min to the launch field), every lock, cash tag, and pin carried over from the plan. Page through the legs; what Janice used to hand-type, now generated — and bookable from paper.

    ＋ new variation opens the fork composer: name it, start blank or copy the current plan — and the decide-by rule is stated up front, derived from whatever cancellation deadlines end up inside.

    The fork lives inside the plan, not in a separate doc: tabs switch the last three days between Option A (all-in Piran, active) and Option B (three towns). The active one fills the page; compare side by side opens both at once.

    Two futures, column by column — differences highlighted: one base vs three towns, a Venice day vs an Istrian crawl, minimal driving vs two borders. Neither is deleted; both hold only refundable bookings, and whichever stays unpicked by May 19 releases itself. They’d later cancel Piran for Trieste — and wish they hadn’t.

    The whole trip in your pocket — no separate "mobile app." Plan stacks one leg per row, Today lands on the next thing with Directions and Call, Book keeps its countdowns and cash, and the grid deals one day per swipe. Same data, one column.
